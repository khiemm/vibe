Bond's opening chase still works because it never feels weightless. You can sense the breath, the missed footing, and the shock of every landing, so the tension comes from physical effort rather than glossy spectacle.

<YouTubeEmbed
  videoId="hEJnMQG9ev8"
  title="Mad Max: Fury Road Official Main Trailer"
  note="Mad Max: Fury Road | Warner Bros. Pictures"
/>

`Mad Max: Fury Road` creates pressure through relentless forward motion without becoming unreadable. Every frame pushes metal, dust, and bodies in the same violent direction, which makes the whole pursuit feel one mistake away from collapse.

<YouTubeEmbed
  videoId="EXeTwQWrcwY"
  title="The Dark Knight Official Trailer"
  note="The Dark Knight | Warner Bros. Entertainment"
/>

What makes `The Dark Knight` memorable is the sheer mass behind its action. When a truck flips in the middle of the street, it does not play like a trick shot; it feels like the city itself is being bent by force.

<YouTubeEmbed
  videoId="ZL9fnVtz_lc"
  title="Heat: Bank Robbery Shootout"
  note="Heat | Movieclips"
/>

`Heat` remains a benchmark because the gunfire, silence, and panic all stay grounded. It does not need frantic cutting; it only needs the camera to hold long enough for every decision to feel expensive.
